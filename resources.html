Implicit Bias Resources

"Implicit Bias" is a term that names the attitudes and stereotypes that can subconsciously shape one's thoughts, actions, and decisions without even realizing it. Whether the associations someone holds are positive or negative, every single person is susceptible to having implicit biases. These biases can be best understood by approaching them with an "intersectional" framework. Coined in 1989 by Kimberl√© Crenshaw, intersectionality helps us understand how different identities and experiences overlap.

To do your part of helping create a more equitable world, please take the time to learn more about implicit bias by exploring the following resources:

  1. Implicit Bias Test

    Harvard University's Implicit Association Test (IAT), lead by Project Implicit, is a key assessment tool for understanding one's unconscious biases.

  2. National Equity Project's Framework on Implicit Bias and Structural Racialization

    This article written by Kathleen Osta and Hugh Vasquez goes into depth about why discussing structural racism is crucial when unpacking implicit bias.

  3. UCLA Office of Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion: Implicit Bias Video Series

    This free series from the University of California, Los Angeles features six videos discussing implicit biases and their affects.

  4. National Research Mentoring Network (NRMN) Unconscious Bias Course

    The NRMN provides professional-level mentorship and programming with a focus on diversity, equity, and inclusion. The Unconsious Bias Course they offer is a multi-module program that awards certifications upon completion of each module.
